# ResolveNet Governance

## Overview

ResolveNet follows a transparent, community-driven governance model aligned with CNCF best practices.

## Roles

### Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for the overall direction and quality of the project. They have merge rights and are responsible for releases.

See [MAINTAINERS.md](MAINTAINERS.md) for the current list.

### Contributors

Anyone who contributes code, documentation, issues, or reviews is a contributor. Contributors are recognized in release notes and the CONTRIBUTORS file.

### Reviewers

Reviewers are trusted contributors who have demonstrated expertise in specific areas and are granted review responsibilities for those areas.

## Decision Making

- **Consensus**: Decisions are made by maintainer consensus.
- **RFCs**: Significant architectural changes require an RFC (see `docs/rfcs/`).
- **Voting**: If consensus cannot be reached, a simple majority vote among maintainers decides.
- **Transparency**: All decisions are documented in GitHub issues, PRs, or RFCs.

## Becoming a Maintainer

1. Sustained, high-quality contributions over a period of time.
2. Demonstrated understanding of the project architecture and goals.
3. Nominated by an existing maintainer.
4. Approved by majority of existing maintainers.

## Code of Conduct

All participants are expected to follow the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
